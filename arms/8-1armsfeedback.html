8.1 Arms Community Feedback Last Updated: October 19, 2018
Marok
LAD NUMBER 2

Overall

Currently, Arms is in a very good spot, both in terms of gameplay and it's competitiveness within raids/Mythic+. It has a versatile toolkit that can adapt to many situations, and rarely feels "weak" or like it's lacking something during a particular encounter. Arms gameplay does lack a bit of depth and can be boring at times, but of course that's up to personal preference and is definitely not necessarily a bad thing. Talent variety/choice is lacking; we run the same build for most content. We have some interesting Azerite traits and many have a decent impact on how the spec is played. Overall, Arms is a fun and competitive spec and isn't in need of any significant changes.

Toolkit/Gameplay

One of Arms' strengths is our versatile toolkit and ability to adapt to many situations. We have the ability to talent into a strong on-demand defensive via Defensive Stance, a powerful raid cooldown (Rallying Cry), as well as AoE crowd control with Intimidating Shout. Arms works very well in most competitive PvE content. We have very strong burst AoE, strong single-target, strong cleave/two-target DPS, and a strong execute phase that requires no ramp up.

There's not a whole lot of negative things to say about Arms gameplay. It's definitely on the simpler side, and as a result can be a bit boring, but that can also be a positive for some. Is this due to talents?

The Value and Scaling of Strength

One concern is the direction Strength seems to be heading in regards to how much its valued item level keeps increasing. Currently, Strength is a "good" stat, but as item level increases, the value of secondary stats increases, and the value of Strength doesn't. Strength has very little interaction with Arms' toolkit/Azerite traits, whereas secondaries do. As such, Strength will get "left behind", and the importance of item level decreases. We'd like to see this addressed so item level in the future still remains important.

Talents

Talents are the primary pain point for Arms. There is very little variety and choice when it comes to talent builds; the "utility rows" offer good choice, but many talents simply aren't competitive and as such we're locked into a single talent for a given row.
